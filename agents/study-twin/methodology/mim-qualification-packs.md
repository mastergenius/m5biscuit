# MIM Qualification StudyPacks

Status: working policy, 2026-05-24.

This note defines how MIM material should be projected into StudyPacks when the goal is not
memorization, but qualification growth: becoming a person who can be trusted with more complex work.

## Source Frame

The motivating source is a MIM-facing claim about the market price of MBA-scale transitions:
expensive business education is bought as a bet that a person can operate at a higher managerial
scale. The useful learning object is not the MBA comparison itself. The useful object is the
qualification question:

```text
What complexity can this learner now be trusted with, and what evidence supports that trust?
```

## Qualification Axes

MIM packs should train and diagnose three axes.

`agency`
: The learner initiates a change in real work rather than only analyzing or commenting.

`scale`
: The learner can hold a wider situation: self, team, organization, community, or ecosystem.

`methodological_discipline`
: The learner uses explicit methods, distinctions, models, checks, and evidence instead of relying
  only on experience, intuition, charisma, or local heroics.

## Scale Ladder

`self`
: Can name and improve their own method of work; can repair misses without drama.

`team`
: Can reduce rework by making roles, work products, descriptions, and done conditions explicit.

`organization`
: Can reason across roles, incentives, constraints, risks, and partial information.

`community`
: Can shape shared language, standards, and reusable methods beyond one team.

## Pack Acceptance Rule

A MIM qualification pack is useful only if it produces at least one of these evidence objects:

- a grounded description of a real work situation;
- a role/interest/constraint map;
- a method/work split;
- a risk and early-signal list;
- a rework-reduction move;
- a qualification claim with supporting evidence;
- a next experiment that changes work within 24 hours.

If the learner only recognizes terms, the pack failed as qualification material.

## Item Families

`complexity_case`
: A situated case with several roles, interests, constraints, incomplete data, and conflicting
  descriptions. The learner must choose a first methodological move.

`trust_claim`
: The learner writes a compact claim: "I can be trusted with X complexity because evidence Y."

`rework_reduction`
: The learner identifies which bad description, missing boundary, or role confusion will create
  rework and proposes a small repair.

`risk_early_signal`
: The learner names how the target system can become unsuccessful and which early signal should be
  watched without turning it into a Goodhart target.

`role_interest_map`
: The learner maps roles, interests, constraints, and decision rights before proposing action.

`evidence_portfolio`
: The learner converts past work into evidence of agency, scale, and method discipline without
  drifting into self-promotion.

## Relation To R3 And R5

R3-oriented packs should focus on ambiguity in descriptions, documents, schemas, responsibilities,
and communication. Their evidence should show reduced rework and shared understanding.

R5-oriented packs should focus on target systems, supersystems, stakeholders, constraints, risks,
and failure modes. Their evidence should show earlier risk detection and better system framing.

Both tracks must end in work: a changed artifact, a better decision surface, a clearer role map, or
a next action in a real project.
