# MIM Guide 1 Self-Development Pack Policy

Status: active generation policy, 2026-05-28.

This note records how the first Guide 1 self-development excerpts are projected into Biscuit
StudyPacks after user feedback on shallow, repetitive packs.

## Feedback Incorporated

- M5Paper should provide propagation and calibration, not long unscaffolded writing.
- Packs should be information-dense enough to feel like a compact guide, not one idea stretched
  across many taps.
- Cases should use plausible alternatives; the right answer must not always be first or surrounded
  by obviously foolish distractors.
- Learner-facing copy should not mix noisy labels like "Case:" or "Mini analysis:" into the content.
- Final prompts should route the next branch instead of repeating a generic reflection.
- Writing-heavy outputs should become workbook/web tasks later; M5Paper should collect routing
  signals and recognition evidence.

## Guide 1 Projection

The first Guide 1 source is projected into five larger branches:

`mim-selfdev-learner-culture`
: Protected slot, self-regulation, thought capture, information hygiene, entry ritual,
  procrastination, and learner-culture audit.

`mim-selfdev-bolid-machine`
: Bolide, Pilot, Machine, exobody, exocortex, method as runtime, feedback loop, and broken links.

`mim-selfdev-worldview-grounding`
: Worldview, phases, fast/slow thinking, formal thought, object/description, grounding,
  systemness/systematicity, and Deutsch optimism.

`mim-selfdev-roles-gates`
: Creator roles, learner metarole, problem/task, work product, frontier, development steps,
  double gate, and bottleneck.

`mim-selfdev-values-trajectory`
: Values, dissatisfactions, intentions, creative conveyor, recovery, balance, caliber, SoTA
  environment, and trajectory project.

Each pack is a runtime projection, not the durable source. Durable source excerpts remain in the
local MIM course workspace. The generated pack should keep source provenance in `manifest.json`.

## Composition Rule

Each Guide 1 M5Paper pack should normally contain:

- 2-4 compact propagation episodes that carry dense conceptual material;
- 6-8 calibration episodes with realistic situations and non-trivial alternatives;
- 1-2 contrast or repair episodes that expose common misuse;
- 1 final routing episode with branch choices.

No episode should require long written production on M5Paper. When a source practice requires a
table, map, dashboard, or one-page synthesis, the M5Paper episode should route to a workbook branch.

## Acceptance

A Guide 1 pack is useful only if after the session the learner can do at least one of these:

- name a bottleneck in their personal development infrastructure;
- distinguish role, method, product, and evidence in a realistic situation;
- identify whether a failure is in Pilot, Machine, feedback, or a broken link;
- ground an abstract concept into object, action, and observable sign;
- choose a next branch: deeper cases, workbook, real application, or repair of basics.

Completion alone is not evidence of usefulness.
